Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about booking, our services, insurance, and what to expect on your ride.

General
What is Non-Emergency Medical Transportation (NEMT)?+
NEMT provides safe, reliable rides to and from medical appointments for patients who don't require an ambulance but can't drive themselves — including dialysis, chemotherapy, physical therapy, and specialist visits. We handle the logistics so patients and families can focus on care.
Is Mendez Transportation licensed and insured?+
Yes. Mendez Transportation LLC is fully licensed as a NEMT provider in the state of Florida and carries commercial liability and passenger insurance. All vehicles meet state safety requirements and are regularly inspected.
Can a family member or caregiver ride with the patient?+
Absolutely. One companion or caregiver may accompany the patient at no additional charge on most trips. Additional passengers may be accommodated depending on vehicle capacity — let us know when booking.
Do you transport children or minors?+
Yes, minors can be transported when accompanied by a parent or legal guardian. For unaccompanied minors, special arrangements must be made in advance. Contact us to discuss your specific needs.
Booking & Scheduling
How far in advance do I need to book?+
We recommend booking at least 24 hours in advance to guarantee availability. For recurring appointments (dialysis, chemo), we offer standing weekly schedules. Same-day rides may be available in Orange and Osceola counties — call us by 8 AM to check.
How do I schedule a recurring appointment?+
Call us or send an email with your recurring schedule and we'll set up a standing booking. Many of our dialysis and chemotherapy patients have standing rides 3 times per week. Once set up, you'll receive confirmation and reminders automatically.
What if I need to cancel or reschedule?+
Please notify us at least 2 hours before your scheduled pickup. Cancellations with less than 2 hours notice may be subject to a cancellation fee. We understand medical needs are unpredictable — just call us as soon as you know.
What if my appointment runs longer than expected?+
No problem. Our drivers wait for you at no extra charge for the first 60 minutes. If your appointment runs longer, simply call or text us and we'll coordinate your return pickup. We understand medical appointments don't always run on schedule.
Medical Needs & Equipment
Are your drivers trained to assist patients?+
Yes. All Mendez Transportation drivers are CPR-certified, background-checked, and trained in passenger assistance techniques. They know how to safely secure wheelchairs, assist ambulatory patients, and handle medical equipment like oxygen tanks.
Can I bring my oxygen tank or medical equipment?+
Yes. We can accommodate portable oxygen concentrators, oxygen tanks, IV poles, and other medical equipment. Please let us know what equipment you'll be bringing when you book so we can arrange the right vehicle.
Do you transport wheelchair and stretcher patients?+
Absolutely. We have wheelchair-accessible vans with ramp and lift systems, certified tie-down equipment for power and manual wheelchairs, and stretcher transport vehicles for patients who must remain lying down. Select your vehicle type when booking.
Can you pick up patients from a hospital or nursing home?+
Yes. We coordinate directly with hospital discharge staff and nursing home personnel to ensure smooth handoffs. We require the facility's address, floor/room number, and a contact name. For hospital discharges, notify us the day before when possible.
Insurance & Payment
Do you accept Medicaid or insurance?+
We work with most major insurance providers and Medicaid plans in Florida. Contact us with your insurance details and we'll verify coverage before your first ride. Florida Medicaid-eligible trips may be fully covered at no cost to you.
What if I don't have insurance?+
We offer competitive private-pay rates for patients without coverage. Contact us for a quote based on your route and service type. We never turn away a patient due to inability to pay without first exploring all options with you.
How does billing work?+
For insurance-covered trips, we bill your provider directly — no upfront payment required in most cases. For private-pay, we accept credit cards, debit cards, and cash. Invoices can be provided for reimbursement if needed.

Still Have Questions?

Our team is available 7 days a week. Call us, send an email, or fill out our contact form and we'll get back to you within 2 hours.
